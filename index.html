About the Project

What is
BetaUp?

BetaUp is a human-centric application designed to enhance the experience of climbers and coaches through gamification, real-time feedback, and social connectivity.

Rooted in the Active Lifestyles theme of CPT208, our project explores how playful design principles can motivate climbers from beginner to advanced level, and help coaches deliver more engaging, data-driven guidance.

We apply a user-centred design process: conducting field research, building personas, prototyping iteratively, and evaluating with real users at climbing venues.

Target Platform Mobile + Web · Flutter & Spring Boot
Good-to-Have Features Delivered Location-based gym discovery · AI Voice Assistant · BLE Heart Rate Monitor

🏅
Achievement & Badge System
Climbers automatically unlock badges on milestones — first send, streaks, grade PRs. Each unlock triggers a celebratory animation, turning every session into a satisfying achievement.
🐼
AI Voice Assistant
A draggable panda avatar activates a full-duplex voice interface. Speak hands-free mid-climb: "Log a V5 flash" or "Start my session" — DeepSeek NLP understands and acts.
🤝
Social & Community Layer
Live leaderboards, community posts, comments, and a follow graph create a motivating social ecosystem. Compete weekly, celebrate sends, and discover training partners nearby.
📍
Location-Aware Gym Discovery
Discover climbing venues nearby, check in, and link session data to a real location. C2 track's location-based Good-to-Have fully realised.
🏫
Coach Certification System
Coaches apply for certified status through the app; admins review and approve or reject applications. Verified coaches unlock a dedicated panel to view athlete data and post training feedback — a complete role-based workflow rarely seen in consumer climbing apps.
❤️
BLE Heart Rate Monitor
Bluetooth LE integration that connects to an Apple Watch or compatible wearable to stream real-time heart rate data during a climbing session, enabling hardware-linked training insights.

01 — Motivation & Research

Why BetaUp?

Understanding the problem space, the literature, existing tools, and the people we are designing for.

The Why — Motivation Statement

We chose rock climbing as our research focus because climbing uniquely combines physical challenge, technical skill, and social interaction. Unlike running or general fitness, climbing demands route-reading, movement planning, and mental composure — creating richer user needs around session logging, technique analysis, progress feedback, and community connection.

Since climbing's Olympic debut at Tokyo 2020, the sport has attracted a growing wave of younger participants. Yet the digital ecosystem still largely prioritises utility over experience — existing apps focus on training records or route databases, while fun, engagement, and playfulness remain underserved.

Several team members are regular gym climbers at XJTLU's campus wall. We found ourselves juggling multiple apps, spreadsheets, and WeChat group chats for what should be one coherent experience — log a send, celebrate progress, get coach feedback, and find routes nearby. BetaUp was built from that frustration, filling the gap between pure utility and genuinely playful daily engagement.

Literature Review — Academic Papers
4 Academic Papers · 3 Strengths + 3 Gaps per source
Paper 3 Strengths 3 Gaps
BoulderPlay: Exploring AR and Vibrotactile Integration for Novel Bouldering Experiences SportsHCI 2025
  • Strong novelty — transforms route completion into a dynamic, interactive game experience
  • Multi-modal interaction (visual AR + haptic feedback) creates a richer, more immersive session
  • Proves climbing apps can be genuinely playful beyond logging, training, and route lookup
  • Still a prototype — not designed for long-term, everyday use
  • Small sample and short session duration — findings are exploratory with limited generalisability
  • Some participants felt it was "a game with climbing elements" rather than real training
Embedding and Generation of Indoor Climbing Routes with Variational Autoencoder arXiv 2020
  • AI-generated route content — system creates content rather than merely displaying it
  • Clear methodology using a standardised MoonBoard grid; reproducible and extensible
  • Generated routes showed a degree of authenticity, validated by real climbers in the app
  • Generation quality is inconsistent — results vary despite overall feasibility
  • Focused on efficiency of route generation, not user experience or engagement
  • Relies on MoonBoard's fixed grid; difficult to generalise to real gym wall layouts
Wearable and Non-Invasive Sensors for Rock Climbing Applications Sensors 2023
  • Comprehensive survey covering respiration, ECG, EMG, eye gaze, NIRS, and hold force sensors
  • Strong framework for science-based training — fatigue, movement efficiency, muscle load analysis
  • Provides quantifiable feedback entry points for building professional-grade climbing tools
  • Heavy hardware dependency — multiple sensor types mean high cost and wearing complexity
  • Useful for performance analysis but offers little for playful engagement or daily retention
  • High deployment barrier — impractical for casual gym users or beginner climbers
Computer Vision Based Indoor Rock Climbing Analysis UCSD Technical Report 2022
  • High automation — identifies holds, colours, pose, and key movement moments from video
  • Strong accuracy: hold detection 96.3% mAP, colour classification 99.3%
  • Significant-frame approach reduces computation while capturing key climbing actions
  • Small dataset — only 53 manually annotated wall images used for training
  • Sensitive to pose estimation errors — body occlusion degrades frame analysis quality
  • Limited robustness to similar-coloured routes; threshold tuning needed per environment
Competitive Analysis — Commercial Products
4 Commercial Benchmarks · 3 Strengths + 3 Gaps per product
Product 3 Strengths 3 Gaps
Vertical-Life
Training / Logbook / Topo vertical-life.info
  • Clear training orientation — suitable for goal-driven users seeking systematic progress
  • Strong progress tracking through ascents, training plans, and historical records
  • Comprehensive: training plans + logbook + topo + route sharing in one platform
  • Too serious — efficiency-focused; casual users and beginners may find it off-putting
  • No playful feedback loop — no daily tasks, exploratory challenges, or surprise mechanics
  • Low fun appeal — users open it to train and record, not for enjoyment
27 Crags
Outdoor Guide / Route Discovery 27crags.com
  • Powerful crag and route search — ideal for pre-trip outdoor planning
  • Practical filtering by location and grade range for fast route discovery
  • Designed around "more climbing, less clicking" — efficient topo experience
  • Purely utility-driven — users open it to find routes, not to have fun
  • Single-task context — used before/at a crag; hard to build daily opening habit
  • No engagement mechanism — no achievement chains, social tasks, or growth narrative
KAYA
Social + Gym Guide + Challenges kayaclimb.com
  • Stronger social layer — partner finding, community beta videos, higher interaction
  • Activity-driven participation — monthly challenges and leagues stimulate return visits
  • Closest to a "fun climbing app" — actively integrates community to increase engagement
  • Fun depends on external events — flat experience when no challenge is active
  • Lacks lightweight daily gamification — no small tasks, exploration rewards, or growth narrative
  • No stable playful loop — drops in engagement when challenges, leagues, or friends are absent
Mountain Project
Route Database / Community Guide mountainproject.com
  • Most comprehensive information — routes, photos, area guides, and community forum
  • High community content value — ideal for route planning, beta lookup, and to-do lists
  • Classic platform with proven, very strong practical utility
  • Feels like a reference tool — experience is information-centred, not entertainment-centred
  • Lacks systematic fun design — has community features but no gamification layer
  • Low playful engagement — users open it to find routes or save to-dos, not because the app is compelling
Key Insight

All four products skew heavily towards utility — they help users climb better, find routes, or manage training data. None delivers a persistent, intrinsically enjoyable daily experience. The common pattern: engagement either depends on external events (challenges, competitions) or collapses once the immediate task is done. BetaUp targets this gap directly — placing gamification, social playfulness, and voice-first interaction at the core so every session feels like an achievement worth opening the app for.

Three Must-Have Playful Features
01
🏅
Badge Collection System
The platform contains four categories of badges: gym-specific badges, achievement badges, training log badges and social interaction badges. Users can unlock badges automatically after completing corresponding behaviours. Exclusive venue badges are available for local climbing gyms, while other badges reward continuous check-ins, completed training records and community engagement. All unlocked badges are displayed on personal profile pages. User research shows that badge rewards are the most popular feature among all user groups. It solves the lack of long-term positive feedback, turns invisible skill improvement into visible collectible goals, and greatly improves user continuous participation and motivation.
Playfulness: immediate positive reinforcement · social display · collection drive
02
💬
Community Post & Social Interaction
Users can publish image posts, climbing partner notices and route strategy guides on the community page. Other users can like, comment and interact with shared content, and tag coaches for professional advice. Users can also visit each other's personal pages to view training records and badge collections. It replaces scattered informal communication on social software, builds a vertical professional climbing community, turns isolated individual training into interactive shared experience, and helps climbers make friends and gain recognition from peers.
Playfulness: peer recognition · shared progress · vertical climbing community
03
🐼
AI Voice Assistant Logging
After each training session, users can simply tap the microphone icon and speak freely about their climbing details. The intelligent voice system automatically recognises professional climbing terms and generates standard structured training logs without manual typing. This feature effectively solves the common pain point where users are too exhausted to record sessions after training. It reduces recording time significantly, lowers operation friction, and makes routine training logging more convenient and enjoyable.
Playfulness: hands-free convenience · conversational interaction · instant feedback

Design Alternatives — Key Decisions
HCI Decision
Manual Form Entry ✓ AI Voice Assistant + BLE Heart Rate (Demo)

Manual form entry is easy to implement but unsuitable mid-climb when hands are occupied. We chose an AI voice assistant — powered by STT and DeepSeek LLM — as the primary hands-free input method. Beyond voice, we also built a BLE heart rate monitoring demo that connects to an Apple Watch or compatible wearable, streaming real-time biometric data into the session view. This demonstrates our vision for deeper hardware integration and quantified training feedback.

Platform Decision
Pure Website ✓ Flutter (Mobile + Web)

The simple web version has many limited functions in actual use, lacking the function of taking photos and notifications, and considering that almost no one climbs with a laptop, we finally chose Flutter for its cross-platform capabilities.

Mechanic Decision
✓ Badges + XP Points Random Loot Boxes

Compared with the clear medal acquisition mechanism, although the random reward box has a sense of surprise, it may not meet the expectations of some users. Additionally, considering that our project is a sports project, there is more motivation with specific goals, so we finally choose the plan of upgrading medals and experience points.

06 — Technical Reflection

AI-Assisted
Development

How we used large language model tools, what we generated, how we verified it, and the ethical considerations.

AI TOOL [1]
Claude Code — Anthropic
claude.ai · accessed April 2026

Used for: Spring Boot API scaffolding (controller, service, repository layers), Flutter screen component generation, JWT authentication boilerplate, database schema design, DeepSeek system prompt engineering, and the voice assistant pipeline — including the VoiceService state machine, DeepSeekClient HTTP wrapper, and the custom CustomPainter panda widget.

Verification approach: Every generated backend endpoint was manually tested via browser or Postman before integration into the Flutter client. Flutter screens were hot-reloaded and exercised on Chrome. Voice assistant state machine edge cases (simultaneous TTS + STT, network failure during DeepSeek call, empty transcripts) were tested by simulating each failure mode.
AI TOOL [2]
DeepSeek Chat API — DeepSeek
api.deepseek.com · accessed April 2026

Used as: The NLP engine inside the voice assistant, parsing natural-language Chinese climbing commands into structured JSON actions — LOG_CLIMB, START_SESSION, END_SESSION, and QUERY_STATS — with typed parameters (grade, venue, result, attempts, notes).

Verification approach: The system prompt was iterated 5+ times to improve robustness. Edge cases explicitly tested: partial commands ("log V5"), ambiguous grades ("blue route"), requests outside app scope ("book a gym slot"), multi-turn context (asking about a session that was just started), and Chinese/English mixed input.

Ethical Considerations

Accessibility

All AI-generated UI components were checked for contrast ratios (minimum 4.5:1 for body text). The voice assistant is a supplementary input method — every voice action is also reachable via touch UI, so the app remains fully usable for users in noisy environments or with speech difficulties.

Language Bias

The DeepSeek NLP model performs better on Chinese commands due to its training data distribution. We document this limitation clearly: non-Chinese speakers may experience lower voice recognition accuracy. A future iteration would support English commands equally.

Data Privacy

No voice audio is stored by BetaUp. Transcribed text is sent to DeepSeek's API for NLP processing; users are informed of this via the microphone permissions dialogue before first use. All session and climb data is stored only in our local H2 database instance.

Code Ownership

AI-generated code was reviewed, understood, and in many cases refactored by the lead developer before integration. We do not submit AI output as-is. The developer is responsible for correctness, security (JWT, SQL safety), and alignment with user requirements.
