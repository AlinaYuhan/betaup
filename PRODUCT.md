# BetaUp — Product Context

## Product Purpose
BetaUp is a rock climbing companion app that integrates gamification, AI voice assistance, community, and coach certification into one cohesive experience. It replaces the fragmented ecosystem (27crags + Crimpd + Strava + WhatsApp) that climbers currently juggle.

## Register
brand

## Users
- **Primary: Alex (recreational climber, 24)** — Climbs 3x/week at a city gym, V4-V5 level, competitive by nature. Wants measurable progress, social validation, coach feedback without friction. Currently uses Notes app + WhatsApp to log sends.
- **Secondary: Coach Li Wei (34)** — IFSC Level 1 certified coach managing 12 intermediate students. Needs a unified dashboard to monitor progress and assign drills without chasing students on WeChat.

## Brand Tone
Bold, energetic, purposeful. This is a sports-tech product for people who hang off walls by their fingertips. Anti-corporate. Anti-generic fitness app. Premium but not luxury — it earns its confidence through specificity and craft.

Anti-references: Strava (generic GPS sports), MyFitnessPal (data-heavy, cold), Notion (productivity tool aesthetic). Nothing that feels like a school project dashboard.

## Core Features
1. **GPS Map** — 700+ climbing venues, sorted by distance, open hours, booking
2. **Session Check-In** — one-tap GPS-confirmed session start
3. **Achievement Badges** — 20+ milestones with animated celebration
4. **AI Voice Assistant (Panda)** — DeepSeek NLP, hands-free climb logging mid-session
5. **Community Feed** — posts, likes, comments, 3 post types (General / Partner Search / Beta Route)
6. **Coach Certification** — in-app coach verification workflow
7. **Apple Watch BLE** — live heart rate during sessions

## Design Principles
- Climbing is visceral and physical — the UI should feel the same
- Every interaction should feel earned, not generic
- Orange (#e8500a) is fire, ambition, the moment a hard route clicks
- Dark backgrounds: climbers train in gyms, often at night, always focused
- Typography does the heavy lifting — Bebas Neue for impact, DM Sans for clarity

## Strategic Context
CPT208 Human-Centric Computing, XJTLU, Group C2-6, 2025-2026. Process Portfolio for academic assessment. Audience: lecturers + peers who understand HCI methodology. The portfolio must demonstrate design thinking, not just a pretty product.
